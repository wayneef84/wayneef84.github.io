# F.O.N.G. PuzzLLer

## What is this?

F.O.N.G. PuzzLLer is a digital sliding-tile logic puzzle designed to test spatial memory and sequential planning.

**Who should play:** Fans of Rubik's cubes, 15-puzzles, and methodical brain-teasers.

**Why it exists:** To provide a robust testing ground for the F.O.N.G. matrix-shifting algorithms, pushing the limits of ES5 array manipulations rendered directly into DOM elements.

---

## Quick Start (30 seconds)

1. Launch the game from the Development section.

2. Choose a grid size (3x3 is the standard starting point).

3. The game will briefly show you the completed image or number sequence, then scramble the tiles.

4. Tap any tile adjacent to the empty "blank" space to slide it into the gap.

5. Continue sliding tiles until the original image or numerical order is restored!

**Pro Tip:** Solve the puzzle row by row, starting from the top. Once the top row is complete, pretend it doesn't exist and solve the remaining sub-grid!

---

## Features

- ✨ **Dynamic Scrambling** - Uses a reverse-walk algorithm to scramble the board, ensuring the puzzle is mathematically solvable 100% of the time.

- 🎮 **Image or Numbers** - Toggle between traditional numbered tiles or a sliced, scrambled image.

- 📱 **Swipe or Tap** - Fully supports tapping individual tiles or swiping across the screen to push multiple tiles at once.

- ⚡ **Move Counter** - Tracks your total moves to encourage optimization and speedrunning.

---

## How to Play

**Objective:**

Restore the scrambled grid to its original, ordered state in the fewest possible moves.

**Rules:**

- You can only move tiles horizontally or vertically into the empty space.

- You cannot move tiles diagonally.

- The puzzle is solved when all numbers (1 through N) are in sequential order, with the blank space in the bottom-right corner.

**Controls:**

- **Tap:** Tap a tile next to the blank space to move it.

- **Swipe (Mobile):** Swipe a row or column toward the blank space to slide all tiles in that line simultaneously.

## Troubleshooting

### Common Issues

**Issue:** The last two tiles are swapped, and I can't fix them.

- **Solution:** If the puzzle was generated by the internal engine, it is mathematically proven to be solvable. You must rotate a larger block of tiles around the grid to fix the parity.

### Known Limitations

- **Custom Images:** Similar to Jigsaw, custom camera uploads are not fully implemented for the sliding tile mode yet.

- **Animations:** Rapid swiping can sometimes cause CSS transition stutters on older devices.
